PRODUCT OVERVIEW — ISL76682

The ISL76682 is a low power, high sensitivity, integrated light sensor with I²C (SMBus Compatible) interface. Its photo-diode array provides close-to human eye spectral response and good IR rejection. The on-chip integrating ADC rejects 50 Hz and 60 Hz flicker from artificial light sources. The lux range is I²C-programmable across four ranges from 0.015 lux/count (Range 1) to 0.96 lux/count (Range 4). AEC-Q100 qualified for automotive ambient light sensing, auto-dimming mirrors, and cluster dimming.

7 · Typical Engineering Workflows
A — Selecting the Correct Lux Range
  1. Determine max expected illuminance (office ~500 lux; outdoor daylight ~10,000 lux; direct sun ~100,000 lux).
  2. Go to Electrical Specifications → ALS Count table. Choose Range 1 (≤1,000 lux), Range 2 (≤4,000 lux), Range 3 (≤16,000 lux), or Range 4 (≤64,000 lux).
  3. Go to Register Set → Bits 1:0 RANGE table. Program bits in Command Register 00h.
  4. Cross-check Integration Time table to confirm no ADC overflow at maximum illuminance.
B — Optimising Integration Time
  1. Go to Integration Time section. Identify target tint for required speed vs resolution trade-off.
  2. Choose REXT and ADC bit depth (n) from the table.
  3. Recommended default: REXT=500kΩ, n=16-bit → tint=100ms. Provides simultaneous 50Hz and 60Hz flicker rejection.
  4. For faster conversion: n=8-bit → tint=390µs at REXT=500kΩ.
C — Verifying Spectral Match to Your Light Source
  1. Go to Charts → Spectral — ALS & IR vs Eye. Drag slider across 450–650nm to confirm ALS closely tracks Human Eye.
  2. Switch to Spectral — Light Sources. Drag slider to the dominant wavelength of your light source to read intensity of all four sources simultaneously.
  3. Cross-reference ALS Linearity to confirm output variation is within ≤±10%.
D — Computing Lux from Raw ADC Count
  1. Configure: EN=1, MODE per application, LIGHT=0 (ALS), RES bits for n-bit resolution, RANGE bits for selected range.
  2. After conversion, read 16-bit DATA: MSB from register 02h, LSB from register 01h.
  3. Apply: Ecal = [Range(k) / 2n] × DATA
  4. Example: Range 1 (1,000 lux), n=16, DATA=20,000 → Ecal = (1,000/65,536) × 20,000 = 305 lux ≈ 300 lux (consistent with DATA_1 typ in Electrical Specs).
E — Setting the I²C Address
  1. Go to Pin Descriptions. Locate Pin 4 (A0).
  2. Tie A0 to GND → I²C address 0x44 (1000100b).
  3. Tie A0 to VDD → I²C address 0x45 (1000101b).
  4. Do not leave A0 floating — address will be undefined.

Typical Workflows

1. Selecting Lux Range

  1. Determine max expected illuminance in your application.
  2. Go to Electrical Specs → ALS Count table. Select Range 1–4.
  3. Program Bits 1:0 of Command Register 00h.

2. Verifying Spectral Match

  1. Go to Spectral — ALS & IR vs Eye. Drag slider to 450–650 nm.
  2. Confirm ALS response closely tracks Human Eye curve.
  3. Use Spectral — Light Sources to check your target light source spectral content.

3. Reading Lux

  1. Read 16-bit DATA from 01h (LSB) + 02h (MSB).
  2. Apply Ecal = [Range(k) / 2n] × DATA
